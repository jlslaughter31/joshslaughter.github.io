## SIEM Introduction!

The next step on the roadmap in my cybersecurity journey is learning about SIEM. I've been working my way through the rooms on TryHackMe. I'm keeping details high-level to avoid spoilers for the millions of people that will for sure read this, and also to stay within platform guidelines.

I just started the Security Solutions section (Cyber Security 101 > Security Solutions) and I'm excited to share that I found it awesome. SIEM collects, normalizes, and analyzes logs from multiple sources to help security teams detect and investigate suspicious activity.

The "Introduction to SIEM" room walked through the basics of SIEM- what it is, what it does, and how it makes life easier on cybersecurity professionals. It really brings it all together when you realize that it brings all the logs from multiple sources (Windows, Linux, etc) and brings them all into one application, formatted, correlated, and easier to see patterns forming in the logs.

---

## My Takeaways

1. SIEM is invaluable. Just from how this room describes SIEM, it is a powerful tool that makes detecting events much simpler than if one were to analyze logs individually.
2. We return to the 3 pillars (People, process, technology)! What is better than the right people (informed and educated on what to look for) with the right process (well-formed rules that trigger alerts) with the technology literally pointing them in the right direction to do their job more effectively. Genius!

---

## What I'd check first in a real investigation:

1. Time window (when did it start?)
2. Who has been affected?
3. Authentication Activity
4. Network Connections made (unusual destinations or ports)


---

## Defender Insights

- Logs are only useful if analysts know how to query and filter them effectively.
- SIEM tools allow analysts to correlate activity across multiple systems.
- Well-written detection rules can reduce noise and help surface real incidents faster.
