## SOC Fundamentals

The next step on the roadmap in my cybersecurity journey is dealing with learning the basics of what an SOC does. I've been working my way through the rooms on TryHackMe. I'm keeping details high-level to avoid spoilers for the millions of people that will for sure read this, and also to stay within platform guidelines.

I'm currently in the Defensive Security section (Cyber Security 101 > Defensive Security) and it's been fun so far! It's been mostly introduction, but it's still cool to kind of see things at work.

This room mostly just explained the 3 Pillars of defense (People, Process, and Technology) and had us open a SIEM to "investigate" a security incident. This was my first look into an SIEM, and even though the room was very structured with step-by-step guidance, it helped me understand how alerts are triaged, gave an overview of how to read logs properly, and showed an example of how to know if something requires further investigation or is planned.

---

## What Clicked for Me

- Tier 1 analysts focus heavily on triage and filtering noise from signal.
- Clear processes matter because investigations must be repeatable and defensible.
- Technology alone does not secure an organization, because people and process determine effectiveness.
- Context determines severity. An alert is just a starting point.
